## §1 Scope

Elastomers are polymers with long-chain flexibility + cross-linking (covalent or physical) → reversible elastic deformation at room T (typical strain-to-fail 200-1000% with negligible permanent set).

Distinct from rigid thermoplastics (GROUP_POL workhorses: nylon, PET, epoxy) because Tg << room T and cross-link density is low enough for rubbery behavior.

## §2 Candidate workhorse elastomers (Phase D coverage targets)

| Polymer | Cross-link | Tg (°C) | σ_f (MPa) | Use |
|---------|-----------|---------|-----------|-----|
| Natural rubber (NR, cis-polyisoprene) | sulfur (vulcanized) | -70 | 20-30 | tire, mount, glove |
| SBR (styrene-butadiene rubber) | sulfur | -55 | 18-25 | tire (75% of synthetic-rubber use) |
| EPDM (ethylene-propylene-diene) | sulfur or peroxide | -50 | 7-20 | weatherstrip, roofing, hose |
| NBR (nitrile rubber) | sulfur | -30 | 12-25 | oil-resistant seal, fuel hose |
| Silicone (PDMS, polydimethylsiloxane) | peroxide or platinum-catalyzed Si-H + Si-vinyl | -125 | 5-10 | implant, sealant, medical |
| Fluorosilicone (FVMQ) | platinum | -65 | 6-10 | aerospace fuel-system seal |
| Polyurethane elastomer (TPU) | physical (urethane H-bond hard segment) | -60 (soft) / 100 (hard) | 30-70 | wheel, conveyor, biomedical |
| Fluoroelastomer (FKM, Viton) | bisphenol or peroxide | -20 | 10-15 | aerospace + chemical-process seal |

## §3 Real-limit anchors (planned)

From `LIMIT_BREAKTHROUGH.md`:
- L1 Frenkel σ_th = E/10 — elastomer E is 1-100 MPa range, so theoretical σ_th 0.1-10 MPa (close to practical for filled rubber)
- L2 practical σ_f — vulcanized NR reaches 20-30 MPa, ~ 2-3× theoretical because of crystallization on stretching (strain-induced crystallization is a uniquely-rubber strengthening mechanism)
